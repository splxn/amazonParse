4.0 out of 5 starsPerfect for work productivity / school
By C on November 30, 2015
Comes with Windows 10 installed and one year of Microsoft office. I didn't bother using the code and installed open office (free, has the same functionality and fully compatible).

Battery life is excellent, streaming video on YouTube is fine.

I really just needed this computer for a few things. 1) telework computer, needed to be cheap and portable 2) something that I can use word/excel on

This computer does both quite well. I open and edit word/excel docs using Open Office while connecting to my office network.

The trackpad does not have gesture capability. So scrolling with two fingers on webpages is out.

I haven't bothered trying to play any games on it. But that's not what I need or want this machine to do.

It's a shade over two pounds so it's very light and easy to throw in a standard messenger bag.

I included a few pics, you can see the difference in screen size compared to my MacBook which has a 13 inch screen. There's also a pic of the keyboard although I cut it off a bit. There's a left and right button under the trackpad. The keyboard is a bit smaller than a standard keyboard so if you have huge hands you may fumble around a bit. There's also a picture of how much free space is on the hard drive right out of the box. This was before I downloaded and installed anything. I did buy a 64 gig micro sd card and I popped that in the side. There's also two 2.0 USB ports in case you want to attach an external hard drive or flash drive.

As I said I needed a cheap light laptop for work. This laptop does more than I need and is perfect for my needs. If the trackpad would scroll id give it 5 stars. When I work I just hook up a mouse, but it'd be nice if I didn't have to bring one everywhere.

By Michael H. Zeldis on November 26, 2015
I actually purchased this computer at Best Buy for $149.99 but since I am a regular Amazon customer I thought I would write a short review.

First the bad.
1. You need to use two hands to scroll which means you have to be sitting down to use it. I guess that is why the call it a laptop. Though it would require balancing in in your lap while using both hands to move around on the monitor. Therefore I would not recommend this computer for a millenial or younger since they probably have zero experience with two handed scrolling.

2. The audio is a tinny but loud enough for newer videos. However, when it comes to playing movies from the 40 or even some TV shows from the last century the audo may not be loud enough for some. The Good
1. It works
2. It boots quickly
3. It will update to Windows 10 TH2 aka 1511. I did it without a problem After the update I used Windows Clean Manager to clean the old Windows Files and was left with 15GB of SSD space. After installing all the programs I am running on my desktops i now have about 13GB of SSD
4. The graphics are good for surfing and running apps such as Libre Office. I am not a gamer so I can no comment on that.
5. I use the two USB ports for two USB external drives which I only connect as needed. One for File History backup and the other for an external USB drive with videos.
