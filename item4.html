5.0 out of 5 starsA great little LED Lamp
By El Kabong on May 20, 2015
Verified Purchase
I was curious about this LED camp lamp, watched it awhile when Amazon had a great price on it during a Daily Deal and jumped. In the past I've had other LED lamps I've tried out, most had the same problem. Cheaply made.

One was so bad, though it worked great light-wise, but was as fragile as an egg and easily broke.

I looked at other LED lamps at REI, and admittedly. coveted them, mainly turned away due to price. Til I ordered the Etekcity lamp.

First off I am very comfortable with the overall construction. Not cheap plastic like some of the others I tried, held . The battery compartment is easily accessable, taking 3 AA batteries. The 30 LEDs are amazingly bright and great for an area lighting. The plunger style system works great, having it light up when lifted up and depending how much light you want, you simply raise or lower the upper assembly. Plus the folding wire handles make it great to carry or hang. The closed overall size of the lamp is roughly the size of a can of soda and a little bit wider. Perfect for camping, fishing, emergency light for a camper/RV/Auto or as an emergency light for home use. Certainly worth the price to afford two or three. Keep one in the trunk of your car, one in the basement or garage and possibly a spare in a closet.

Suggestions for the company, maybe make them availabe in other colors.

5.0 out of 5 starsOw!
By SuperReviewR on July 3, 2015
Ow. That's what I thought when I first turned on this lantern. Lol. This lantern is great for camping or "glamping". It has handles that you can use to carry to the bathroom with you (if you're at a car camping site) or to hang from a tree. You can obviously put it on the table because the surface of the lantern is flat on the bottom. It is plenty bright to light up a bathroom at a campsite and be able to see clearly enough that you can do your business w/o accidentally falling in. Actually the site I was staying at was kinda yucky and the bathroom light was non-existent. Sure I could use the moonlight, but I really wasn't happy with that idea. Luckily, I brought this lantern with me and found a spot (thanks to the handles) to hang it on so that I could safely use the bathroom and also scare off any critters that might be hanging around (it was a raccoon infested site. I like that although it's smaller than my Coleman lantern, it didn't disappoint with the amount of light output.
