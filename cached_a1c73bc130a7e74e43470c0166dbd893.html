5.0 out of 5 starsI am shocked at how fast and responsive it is, especially for a Celeron (quad-core) processor
By Amazon Addict on February 21, 2016
Verified Purchase
Purchased a few of these on Amazon's WOOT site last week, and honestly, I am shocked at how fast and responsive it is, especially for a Celeron (quad-core) processor. Loaded up Photoshop and Office and it's almost as fast as our fourth-gen i5. I've edited large JPG files with several filters, and I've yet to experience any hesitation or choking, even with several apps open. Four processor cores seem to handle the load quite well.

At just over 11 inches, the screen is small, but there's an HDMI out (no VGA) that will display on LCD televisions up to 100 inches or projectors if you need that sort of thing. The keyboard is surprisingly large, accommodating my chubby man fingers quite well. There is no space for a numeric keypad.

And storage is paltry with a 128 GB SSD, but boot times are under 15 seconds, which is mind-blowing. I've done tests on SSD vs traditional hard discs, and surprisingly, besides incredible boot times, I can't find a meaningful difference in overall performance. Two bonuses are an accidental drop won't kill a SSD, and power usage is much more conservative because there are no moving parts. If you do need additional storage, you can opt for external USB 3 hard drives or flash drives, or to conserve space, there is an SD slot on the side which supports a 256 GB SD card (get a fast one - Lexar Professional 1000x 256GB SDXC UHS-II/U3 Card (Up to 150MB/s read) w/Image Rescue 5 Software LSD256CRBNA1000 or data transfer performance may be slow). There are two USB ports, which is generous for such a small unit.Read more ›

5.0 out of 5 starsThis little laptop exceeds expectations!
By Krik on February 27, 2016
This little laptop is a joy to work with! We've looked around, and it blows other options at this price point (I got the $269 Woot deal) out of the water in most aspects.

Good: fastest current mobile Celeron N2000 processor, and although I can definitely tell it's not as smooth as the i5 and i7 systems I have with "heavy" web content, it's more than snappy enough for what we intend to use it for (browsing, Office, light work tasks). 4GB RAM has been doing well, and it's upgradable to 8GB if you need. The 128GB SSD keeps things from bogging down, and you can use the SD card slot to easily and cheaply add 256GB (or you could use the USB3 slot also). The keyboard is very nice, with actual raised keys and good tactile feel. Screen and sound quality are very nice. Networking is AC dual band, Bluetooth, and it even has an ethernet port. It's sturdy and doesn't seem like it would damage too easily.

Less good: relatively low screen resolution and size. Although it would have been more costly, a 12" or 13" screen with smaller bezel would be the thing that made this a "six star" laptop. It's also a bit heavy for its size, but that's what you get if you want something not so flimsy. Did I mention the screen is small?

It does not come with a lot of bloatware preinstalled. You get the usual so-many month subscription to McAfee, which luckily is easy to remove. It also has various rather useless Lenovo applications, and you can tell it's a super-standard build: even though the laptop has an SSD, it comes with an "active protection" agent meant for HDDs turned on. I find it pretty easy with modern Windows to remove/disable these things.

Added a picture of the insides: the RAM, SSD, and battery are all replaceable. Might even be able to upgrade the processor when newer Celerons come out, but I didn't feel like taking off the heat sink just to see if it's a socketed processor.
