5.0 out of 5 starsManage your expectations for the $250 computer, and you will enjoy it more than you think you would
By Kevin Yoo on February 19, 2016
I found my MacBook Pro 15" very heavy to carry around. I needed a computer every day for my school, and found that it was a burden on my back (Quite literally), as well as cumbersome to take it in and out of my back pack every time I needed it.

I then looked for an 11 or 13 inch laptop with the following criteria - reasonable pricing, good battery life, sturdy build, good keyboard, and upgradability. The MacBook Air, while tempting, was off the list immediately - the battery life is amazing, the keyboard is one of the best, and the build is OK, but there is no upgradability, and the price was way too much for what I was looking for.

I then started to browse through Amazon to see what I can find, and lo and behold, I came across this computer. At this price, the ThinkPad 11E offers specs and a build quality that not even $400-500 computers can compete with. Why? Well let me go through this as succinctly as I can:

+) The build quality is phenomenal. Lenovo boasts that this computer can be run over twice by a car, can have a hammer swung at its base and top, and be dropped from 5-6 feet above the ground - no damage whatsoever to the computer's performance. Can you say that about a $800-1000 MacBook Air? The whole feel of the computer feels very tightly built - there are no loose parts that would lead you to believe that this computer will break down anytime soon.
+) The battery life is great. I got around 6-7 hours with 50-70% brightness, and for what I bought this computer for (Light web browsing, using Evernote/Anki/DropBox), it exceeded my expectations
