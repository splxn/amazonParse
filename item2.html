5.0 out of 5 starsOverall, I love the camera and I highly recommend it.
By Jared L Rodriguez on August 1, 2015
Style Name: With Image Stabilization (1080p / 60fps)Package Type: Standard Packaging
I consider myself a Garmin fanatic (I have the Garmin Forerunner 220, Garmin Edge 810, GPSMAP64s, a Garmin vehicle navigator and the Garmin VivoActive [Smart Watch]). I own a GoPro Silver (2014 model) and I just recently purchased the Garmin Virb XE. Overall, I love the camera and I highly recommend it. Heres a breakdown of my thoughts on the Garmin Virb XE.

THE AWESOME STUFF:
1) I LOVE the Garmin VIRB Edit software. I use it on my iMac and it works very well! It did freeze up once, but, when I restarted the program it had automatically saved all of my work. IT'S FREE AND IT'S 10x BETTER THAN THE GOPRO SOFTWARE. (I really hate that GoPro Studio has you cut up and convert all of your video clips.) The Garmin VIRB Edit software is a little hard to use, especially when it comes to syncing data (G-Metrix) to videos. I export my files through Garmin Connect and then upload them to the software with each individual clip.

The Garmin Virb XE keeps a data log (.fit file) in sync with your video recordings so applying the G-Metrix data to the clip is easy (because it's already synced). However, the .fit file has very basic data.

If you have a device like a Garmin Edge 810 that connects with the camera, I would recommend you pair the camera to the device. When you upload your .gpx file (from the Garmin Edge 810 device) to the video it automatically syncs the video to your data. Basically, the .fit file the camera creates is worthless to me.

Do you absolutely need a Garmin Virb XE to add data to your videos? No. I have created videos with GoPro cameras with the Garmin VIRB Edit software, but, it takes me twice as long because syncing the data to the videos is quite a hassel.Read more ›

5.0 out of 5 starsGreat little camera in a compact package
By RF Oregon on August 7, 2015
Style Name: With Image Stabilization (1080p / 60fps)Package Type: Standard Packaging Verified Purchase
UPDATE: 9/6/15: What is it with battery life on any action camera? Taken this thing out a couple more times and keeping it in highest quality mode and last use only allowed me 45 minutes of shooting. Going to have to hook it back up to a backup battery and on the look out for a 3rd party battery for it. Otherwise, video still coming out great, lots of fun action shots and have not had any problem with GPS connectivity or connectivity to iPhone. Keeping it at 5 stars since battery issues seem to be a problem for a lot of cameras and the end product with this thing is still great.

ORIGINAL REVIEW:
I am very impressed with this camera. I have taken it out a couple of times already and have not had any difficulty getting a GPS lock, exactly what I would expect from one of the leaders in GPS equipment. The video it shot in 1080p at 30fps was sharp and putting the gauges on the video was super easy using the free software. I came from a contour camera to this and the difference is night and day. Didn't require that I format my SD card and just uploaded my video with intuitive editing software. Very nice and a massive change from contour, which hit or miss would even recognize the camera! The only con so far is that the little quick start manual is pretty useless. It takes a little bit of toying around with it to figure out all the tricks this compact thing can perform. Looking to putting it through the tests on the mountain bike and hopefully in the water soon. Unlike the other reviewer, I was able to find all the mounts I needed for this quickly on Amazon and Garmin's website. Will update as I get a bit more experience with it!

4.0 out of 5 starsVirb XE preferred to GoPro 3 because of G-Metrix
By Bond on August 18, 2015
Style Name: With Image Stabilization (1080p / 60fps)Package Type: Standard Packaging Verified Purchase
I agree with DC Rainmaker that 4K capture should be included. Battery is extremely tight and hard to remove. Micro SD card is also a struggle to remove. Battery would only charge to two bars after 14 hours and became near hot. Software update 3.4 seemed to correct this problem. Camera now enters a charge mode with no indication of charging or when charge is complete. Hate having to use the camera to charge the battery. Perhaps a separate charging unit is coming. I prefer the Virb latch system over GoPro. The move from GoPro to Virb for me is the G-Metrix which overshadows less than perfect manufacturing. I use the camera for motorcycling. Having speed, elevation, temperature, heading, and track on the screen is terrific. It is all smooth and synced together. The measure of gravity force is mostly useless on a motorcycle because of constant shock/bumps. Capture is darker - somewhat like Gotham City compared to GoPro. Capture in 1080p 60fps is comparable to GoPro. Audio is as good or better than GoPro. Menu is easier to navigate than GoPro and I am able to control the Virb with Garmin Montana. However, the GoPro remote duplicates the camera screen providing more information that just record on/off display of the Virb application for Montana. Virb beep is extremely soft to the point of being useless. There is no volume adjustment. Camera needs an anchor for a safety cord. Uses all my GoPro mounts. Unit is heavier than the GoPro, but not a problem. Virb Edit software is easy enough to use without instruction and well done for the price. I have five garmin products plus maps. Over the years leaning on Garmin for support sends me on my ear. Only one time has Garmin been able to correct a problem. I have found support Garmin's weakness. Maybe they should hire a second engineer. Would like to see GoPro get into GPS hoping the competition will get Garmin off the couch. For now, the GoPro sits. Virb is more of what I want in video capture.
