2.0 out of 5 starsI got what I paid for I guess
By Christna Fredrick Kim on March 13, 2015
Color: Silver Verified Purchase
I had the phone a total of 1 day before it all went to heck. I was happy with the size and crispness of the screen, the fluid and snappy quick feel of going from app to app, the responsiveness to my touch, that it wasn't just stock android 4.2, and the function of the phone especially the camera. I ran a GPS app and it managed to pick up between 12-15 satellites! I thought that for what I paid it wasn't bad but then I also noticed the bad.

What I didn't like was that the phone felt very very cheap. It's all plastic and the the back cover is super flimsy with cheap clips. I was very afraid to break them by opening and closing the back but I was even more afraid of bending the phone just by keeping it in my pocket. The battery lasted a whole day of moderate use of the usual stuff (web, talk, text, music, and games). The back camera is 13 mp but everything came out blurry and washed out. I went up and down in settings trying to fix it but nothing helped it look clear. I wasn't expecting crisp but I wanted some clarity to features. The video recording isn't as smooth as I would like it. There were some stuttering problems of playing back what I recorded but watching videos on youtube was actually nice. It's also running android 4.2 so it's a couple generations behind and after searching the web, I don't think it's going to get 4.4 even though the next android is 5.0. It comes pre loaded with apps but nothing that the normal person wouldn't download themselves. The storage was perfect for what I needed so it's too bad the phone died on me. YOU ONLY GET 2G in the United States! So beware. That is a huge draw back!

It's a budget phone so I didn't go into it thinking it's going to be a major player in the android ring and I wasn't disappointed but it could have lasted longer than 1 day. I still want to try other Lenovo models because with some hard work they might have something but this one didn't do it for me. I say pass on it.

I liked aspects about the phone but ended up returning it for a full refund minus cost of return shipping. Refund process was thankfully a breeze.

Testing out the Lenovo S8 S898T with T-Mobile, I had no issues sending and receiving texts and phone calls. The WiFi also worked perfectly well. My biggest issue came with the data which was unbearably slow/nearly nonexistent. I did manage to receive about three incoming Skype messages and one text from a texting app (all sent to me by my sister in the same room), but this was few of numerous msgs and calls we sent via data & WiFi, most of which were not received. I also tried to send msgs on this Lenovo phone via data from another location... none were received until I connected to WiFi later.

Testing the GPS, it would only locate my current location maybe 1/4 of the time, was often slow to determine a route, and almost always immediately lost signal upon beginning its suggested routes. In the rare times it directed me in real time, it was inaccurate about where I was or was going (and only successfully navigated me around my neighborhood once).
