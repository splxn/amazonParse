5.0 out of 5 starsI can attest that installing Ubuntu on this laptop was easy, and all hardware seems to work without issue
By Scott Reichelt on February 15, 2016
Verified Purchase
The main reason I purchased this laptop was its proposed compatibility with GNU/Linux Ubuntu 14.04 LTS. I can attest that installing Ubuntu on this laptop was easy, and all hardware seems to work without issue. I use this laptop while at school (in the computer science department) and it has been great. The battery life is commendable, and the size of the laptop is appropriate for that setting. The keyboard does not feel too cramped, and has that award winning lenovo feel (concave keys, nice spread, etc).

Their are a couple of options that I would have preferred on this machine, but for the price I can't really complain. The screen resolution is fine, but I would have preferred the optional 1080p screen. This model does not have a back lit keyboard (2016 L450's exist with this option, but are actually kind of hard to come by)

The Fn and Ctrl keys are inverted from what many non-Mac users are used to (Fn is the furthest left key on the bottom row). This can easily by swapped in the BIOS.

The battery is a bit awkward. The laptop does not sit flush, as the battery has a full length bulge in it. The battery is removable, I am sure an alternate battery from lenovo could be swapped out that sits flush.

The warranty on the laptop I received had already ~ 3 months of time ticked off of it, pretty annoying but that has more to do with the seller then the laptop.

To replace my Air, I took a look at the Lenovo laptop offerings. Frankly, I was getting more and more annoyed with the iTunes,photos, and lack of compatibility with my cell phone (Samsung Note 4).

Not all things worked properly. I didn't have any data files or applications installed, so I decided to do a clean install.
