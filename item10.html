4.0 out of 5 starsCHECK your cable SIGNAL strength FIRST
By MysticJaguar VINE VOICE on August 25, 2015
Vine Customer Review of Free Product ( What's this? )
This is a solid cable modem. Setup is easy. Simply put, this modem works and there is no [major] reason not to buy it.

There are two minor points that make it just ever so slightly not up to snuff with the more polished MOT/Arris SB6141.

A) reset time - This modem seems to take 2x-3x to be ready after a power start than other Motorola modems like SB5101U and [Arris] SB6141.
B) heat - The modem is giving off noticeably more heat that the above mentioned modems.

It is slightly larger than the SB 6141 but smaller than the SB5101U.

Either would be fine in plain sight, on a desk, etc. If function is more important than aesthetics, then this modem is fine. If you want a slightly smaller, faster to boot, cooler, and better looking product, go with the ARRIS SURFboard SB6141 DOCSIS 3.0 Cable Modem - Retail Packaging - White

*** CHECK YOUR EXISTING SIGNAL STRENGTH FIRST ***

Often DOCSIS 3.0 modems needs better line quality/strength than DOCSIS 2.0 modems.

On many cable modems you can use 192.168.100.1 to get to the console. Check the following values:

- Downstream Signal to Noise ratio should be >= 33db (if anything is below 33db you are asking for issues)
- Downstream Power Level should be around 0 (anything in the minus range is going to be an issue, double digits minus then forget it)
- Upstream Power Level should be ~40dbmv or slightly below (higher would indicate the modem is trying to shout louder on a weak connection).

If you don't have enough signal a DOCSIS 3.0 modem may not work or at very slow speeds.Read more ›
