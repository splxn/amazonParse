5.0 out of 5 starsAfter 90 days of usage...I am definitely pleased with this item.
By Peninsula Cliff on December 30, 2014
Verified Purchase
Admittedly, I don't use it as a phone but rather as a versatile PDA with computer functions. It is the type of device that I would have preferred in 2011 when I got my Galaxy Tab (7.0: Froyo => Gingerbread), but this smaller size was generally unavailable at that time. I use WiFi exclusively - so, no phone or data bills; but Walmart's StraightTalk phone or tablet kit I'm told would work well.

Speed and responsiveness can only be described as fast...fast...fast!

The quality is unmistakeably IBM, Lenovo's godfather. Screen definition is excellent (how good does it have to be for a 5.5 inch display?). Customer service has not been an issue, since by now I am fairly familiar with how these devices function.

One thing to be aware of is the small amount of disk space available (~2.0-2.2 GB net). So, efficient use of space is necessary and applications should be installed to the SD card when possible.

Also, battery life for me has been excellent: Reducing the display brightness and stopping apps from running unless needed - including Google Play services - helps in this regard.

The only negative for my personal use is the lack of the USB port being OTG-enabled (e.g., I would like to use an USB external mic - but not an absolute requirement); however, meeting this marginal function would have cost me more, or required an undesireable model or device. (I was able to download a Windows .exe program from the Lenovo or XDA website, that may be helpful toward enabling this port.)

This device is shipped with a nice but thin folding wallet-type cover and screen guard. After much searching, shopping, and comparing with nearly everything in my budget ($200. Max!); I hope I have conveyed that I am thoroughly delighted with this purchase.
