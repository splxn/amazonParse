1.0 out of 5 starsTrackpad makes this a no-go. Avoid.
By Doug Thacker on July 30, 2014
The trackpad/touchpad on this machine is so poorly, horribly implemented that it completely fouls the whole experience. Apparently Lenovo has attempted to copy the Apple trackpad, but without knowing how to (either that, or they are simply too big to care). The buttons have been removed from the trackpad, and a touch works the same as a click. The result here is that most attempts to left click result instead in a right click, while attempts to highlight and copy usually require three or four careful efforts. Additionally, when you are scrolling and try to reposition your finger on the pad, it usually results in an inadvertent click when your finger comes into contact with the pad. This machine is rated two out of five stars on Lenovo's own site, and if you read the reviews there the trackpad is the reason.

We are way too far into this technology to get something so fundamental so wrong. Additionally, there is only one dimm slot for ram. The max you can get on this machine is 8 GB of ram, which is soldered onto the board. Why? Its predecessor (X230) had 16 GB - so here we have a downgrade. I'm sure if you're using this machine with a mouse - and don't mind the limited ram - it's perfectly fine (and if you don't mind the mound of obnoxious bloatware it comes pre-packaged with); but I travel a lot and don't want to do that. Why should I have to? (My X240 came with Windows 8 - which I've since upgraded to 8.1 - making the trackpad problems even more pronounced.)

I bought Lenovo Thinkpad because I thought it was the one PC brand I could count on. I was wrong, and because of the trackpad this machine is the worst laptop I've ever owned. I regret the purchase and will be replacing it ASAP.

5.0 out of 5 starsBest laptop I have owned
By nextstep99 on August 3, 2014
Verified Purchase
I know there have been mixed reviews on the X240, especially from long time Lenovo ThinkPad owners.
I was looking for a powerful laptop that I can hook up to a monitor, keyboard and mouse while at home, but be small enough to easily travel with and decided on this one.
One of the major issues people seem to have is with the track-pad. I don't use track-pad buttons very often, preferring to double tap the pad. I still prefer a mouse, but the track-pad works ok for me, when I'm not using the mouse.
The two USB ports could be an issue, but I purchased a small Bluetooth mouse for when I am away from home and it leaves both USB ports free for a hard drive or flash drive.
I like the keyboard, which has a much better feel than my Toshiba, but as I'm not an accomplished typist, the TrackPoint doesn't get much use.
8GB of RAM is enough for what I do, (it would be nice to be upgradeable, but that option seems to be disappearing on ultra-books) and the processor, when teamed with the SSD, moves very quickly.
I'm happy with the standard battery. I get excellent battery life (my recent Toshiba was good for 3 hours when new...) I didn't want the bulk of the upgraded battery, although some reviews like the keyboard lift (and huge boost in battery time) it gives. It is nice to have the option of easily adding a battery, that some other manufacturers no longer allow
I wanted Windows 7 (it came with the pro version), and I didn't want a touch screen. I've tried Win8 and I have to say I'm not a huge fan, but I guess I can get used to it sometime in the future.
