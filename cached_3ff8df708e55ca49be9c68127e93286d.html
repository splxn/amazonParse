5.0 out of 5 starsLenovo's Warrior is a Smart Choice
By Dante Cincilus on December 30, 2014
Color: Standard Verified Purchase
If I could give half a star, I would rate the phone with 4.5 stars; giving it a 4 seems too low. First, keep in mind that this is a 'mid-range' phone and I rate the phone as such. There is no LTE, no NFC, no Infrared blaster, so this phone does NOT stand with the likes of a Galaxy Note 4 or LG G3 or HTC M8, nor does it pretend to be. That said, the phone is still amazing and it will not disappoint. To start, the device is powered by a true octa-core processor thrumming away at 1.4GHz assisted by 2GB RAM and an acceptable Mali-450 GPU. It was able to run my games seamlessly [Castle Clash and Marvel Champions being my favorites]. Video chatting via Hangouts as well as selfies are crisp with the front-facing 5MP camera. The screen offers an HD resolution of 720p. Running video side by side on VLC with my Galaxy Note 3 [1080p], I could barely see the difference in image quality. Colors are vibrant and this especially shows when taking pictures with the camera. There is also less of a battle with auto-focus (an issue with the Samsung GN3) and the rear 13MP shooter snaps movements with ease. Storage is not an issue as it starts you off with a good 14GB after the system gulps its share. You can expand this easily with the mini-SD slot. The unlocked dual-SIM feature is great. You can have literally have two active numbers and choose from which you want to call or text from. The only issue I have is one will only offer 2G data speeds. There is no LTE, but the HSPA will give you download speeds of over 30Mbps. There is no NFC, so no paying with your phone via Google Wallet or sharing photos by placing devices back to back. There is WiFi direct and you can use the phone as a mobile hotspot out-of-the-box without paying extra. The UI runs atop Android Jelly Bean 4.Read more ›

4.0 out of 5 starsHas it's flaws but for the price I can live with it.
By JayCee on April 4, 2015
Color: Standard Verified Purchase
I just got this phone and I have ony been using it 3 days now so I will update this review after more time. The phone got to me in good condition and it is aesthetically good looking. It is only just smaller than the Samsung Note 2, in fact I have it in my note 2 folio case. It is a bit loose in there but it doesn't fall out. Lenovo is a known brand in the PC market (IBM) but I think they made this phone specifically for China. First of all, even though I have the phone language set to English some things still display in Chinese. It is not enough for you not to be able to figure everything out though. One or two things also have grammatical errors such as would be written by someone whose first language is not English.

The phone comes loaded with a number of apps, a speed booster, battery saver, some nice wallpapers, browsers, a cache cleaner, etc. The screen is very clear and the resolution is good. In researching this phone I saw that in benchmarks it was just only slightly above the Note II even though it has a 8 core processor. The phone does not perform anything near to how the Note II performs though. The screen is large and easy to see. The touch screen is not nearly as responsive, it freezes at times, sometimes you will press something and you will think you did not click it because of how long the phone takes to respond. Sometimes the phone does not respond at all, several times I have tried to swipe down to pull down the notifications and it did not respond at all. I have had to reboot the phone several times per day.

The battery is just ok, it is not nearly as powerful as the battery in the Note II, this is a 2000mAh battery and the Note II is a 3100mAh battery. The battery will last you most of the day with light to moderate use.Read more ›
