4.0 out of 5 starsFair priced laptop with satisfactory performance
By Rogelio Perea on January 15, 2016
Was looking for a no frills laptop while on trip (left my powerhowse Lenovo far away at home) and checking the 300-400 US dollars offerings I settled on the Lenovo ideapad 500 with an AMD A10 processor. It does what I need on the road quite swiftly ONCE all the latest updates to Windows 10 are installed. Connected through a hotel WiFi network it took me about 3 hours to have all MS updates installed and the laptop configured as I needed, which implies removing a lot of the bundled stuff these come with and installing essentials like Notepd++, Paint.NET, FileZilla and of course Firefox. After that my work on the road has been a boon, quite pleased with the performance of the ideaplus.

On the cons side I would list the keyboard, it's a bit mushy but all things accounted for it is pretty much what is available on laptops on this price range. What drew me into this computer was the 8G ram and 1T hard drive, the display clarity and size were also big pluses there. Have not used it extensively on battery power but the few times I have the battery life has been satisfactory - have kept it unplugged from AC for about two hours and kept above 50% left over before plugging in the charger.

So it is not a MacBook nor the high end Lenovos, but it will get the job done at a price and performance that's hard to beat.
